Start with the exact furniture requirement

Custom furniture can mean a wardrobe, bed, shoe rack, TV unit, mandir, office desk, reception counter or full room storage. Describe the item clearly before asking for pricing. A vague request produces a vague quote.

Measurements you should share

  • Wall width, height and available depth.
  • Door, window, switchboard and skirting positions.
  • Floor condition and any uneven wall issue.
  • Existing furniture that must stay in the room.
  • Photos from front and both side angles.

Questions to ask before paying advance

QuestionWhy it matters
Which material and thickness are included?Prevents comparing two very different scopes as if they are equal.
Which finish is included?Laminate, acrylic, veneer, PU and texture options carry different costs.
Which hardware is included?Hinges, channels, sliding systems and handles affect daily use.
What warranty support applies?Warranty should be understood across eligible products, hardware and workmanship scope.
What is excluded?Transport, installation, electrical, wall repair or lighting may need separate clarity.

Nearby is useful, but process matters more

A nearby furniture maker can visit faster, but location alone does not guarantee quality. Look for clear measurement, material explanation, clean installation, practical design advice and a written quote. Good communication reduces confusion during execution.
