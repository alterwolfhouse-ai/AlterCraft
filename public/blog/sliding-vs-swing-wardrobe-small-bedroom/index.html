Wardrobe Planning

Sliding vs swing wardrobe for a small bedroom

Small bedrooms need careful clearance planning. The wrong shutter type can make a wardrobe difficult to use even when the storage volume is technically enough.

Choose sliding doors when

  • The bed is close to the wardrobe and door swing space is limited.
  • You want a clean, continuous front with mirror or laminate shutters.
  • The wardrobe is wide enough for overlapping shutters to make sense.

Choose swing doors when

  • You want full access to the complete wardrobe at one time.
  • The room has enough front clearance for open shutters.
  • You want easier hardware maintenance and lower starting cost.

Sliding wardrobes feel premium in compact rooms, but tracks need good hardware and regular cleaning. Swing wardrobes are simpler, but the shutter swing must not hit the bed, study table or side table.
