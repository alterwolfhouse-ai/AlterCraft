Start with layout, not finish

A straight kitchen, L-shaped kitchen, parallel kitchen and island kitchen do not use the same amount of cabinetry or hardware. Parallel and L-shaped layouts usually need more corner planning, more shutters and more counter coordination. Island layouts can look premium, but they add countertop, electrical and circulation planning that must be measured carefully.

Before asking for a price, note the wall lengths, ceiling height, sink location, hob location, refrigerator position and whether tall storage is required. This makes the first estimate sharper and reduces changes later.

Cabinet material and moisture exposure

Delhi NCR kitchens deal with cooking heat, sink moisture, daily cleaning and seasonal humidity. The cabinet material must be selected for the zone. Sink base units and utility-side cabinets need more moisture-aware thinking than dry wall units. BWR or BWP plywood options, HDHMR, pre-laminated boards and engineered boards each have a different use case.

Practical rule: do not choose material only from a showroom sample. Choose it by where the cabinet will sit, how often it will be cleaned, and how much load the unit will carry.

Finish choice changes both look and maintenance

Laminate remains practical for many Indian homes because it is durable and easy to maintain. Acrylic gives a glossy premium finish but needs more careful cleaning. Veneer looks rich and warm, but it belongs in zones where natural texture is the priority. PU, membrane and glass shutters can also change the estimate depending on design intent.

The best kitchen design with price conversation should compare finish, warranty support, cleaning habits and repairability, not only the first-day look.

Hardware is where daily comfort comes from

Soft-close hinges, tandem drawers, lift-up shutters, pull-out baskets, corner units, pantry systems and tall units can make a kitchen easier to use. They also change cost. If the budget is tight, prioritize hardware where daily movement is highest: cutlery, plates, cookware, oil bottles, dustbin and pantry access.

A kitchen that opens smoothly every day feels more premium than a kitchen that only looks expensive in photos.

Countertop, sink and appliances need coordination

Granite, quartz and solid-surface counters each affect cost and maintenance. The countertop must coordinate with hob cutout, sink cutout, chimney position, tile height and appliance clearances. Built-in ovens, dishwashers, microwaves and refrigerators need clear dimensions before fabrication.

If appliance decisions are delayed, the kitchen may need rework. Share appliance model sizes during measurement whenever possible.

How to prepare for a better estimate

  • Send a straight photo of every kitchen wall.
  • Measure wall width, ceiling height and window position.
  • Mark sink, gas, exhaust, chimney and electrical points.
  • List appliances: hob, chimney, microwave, oven, dishwasher and refrigerator.
  • Decide whether you need tall storage, pantry unit or loft storage.
  • Share preferred finish: laminate, acrylic, veneer, PU or mixed finish.
