What a clear quote should show

  • Layout type and approximate cabinet sizes.
  • Material grade for base, wall and tall units.
  • Shutter finish and edge banding details.
  • Hardware brand or quality level.
  • Countertop, sink and accessories scope.
  • Installation, delivery and site-work assumptions.
  • Warranty support across eligible products and workmanship.

Watch for missing scope

A lower quote may exclude countertop, appliances, old kitchen dismantling, plumbing, electrical, chimney ducting, tall units, premium drawers or corner hardware. That does not automatically make it bad, but it must be clear. Hidden exclusions create conflict later.

Do not choose only from a render

Renders help visualize finish, but pricing comes from measured cabinet sizes, material and hardware. Ask for both: a design direction and a written scope. If a render shows tall pantry, profile shutters, under-cabinet lights and accessories, the quote should mention them clearly.

Frequently asked questions

Why do modular kitchen prices vary so much?

Because layout, material, finish, hardware, accessories, countertop and site work vary from one kitchen to another.

Should I trust a one-line kitchen price?

Only as a rough starting point. A reliable price needs written scope and site measurement.

Can I use this guide before a site visit?

Yes. Use it to prepare photos, questions and expectations before requesting a quotation.
