What custom furniture includes

Custom furniture can include wardrobes, storage beds, hydraulic beds, shoe racks, TV units, mandir units, study tables, counters, office storage, workstations and special built-to-size units. The price changes because every piece has a different size, load and detail level.

Main cost drivers

Size and depth

More material, more shutters, deeper storage and tall units increase the scope.

Material and finish

Laminate, acrylic, veneer, PU, mirror and textured finishes all affect the final quote.

Hardware

Hinges, channels, handles, sliders, hydraulic systems and lift mechanisms change cost and usability.

Complexity

Curves, lighting, wall panels, hidden wiring and custom compartments add planning and production time.

Why photos and dimensions matter

A custom quote becomes clearer when the team knows wall size, height, floor condition, plug points, door swing, window position and any existing furniture. Without these details, the first number may be too vague to compare properly.

AlterCraft can begin with photos and approximate measurements, but final execution should always be confirmed with proper measurement and written scope.

How to compare two furniture quotes

  • Check material specification and thickness.
  • Check finish type, color and edge detail.
  • Ask which hardware is included.
  • Confirm delivery, installation and site fitting scope.
  • Ask whether drawers, lofts, mirrors and lighting are included or extra.
  • Confirm warranty support across eligible products, hardware and workmanship scope.

Best first projects for budget control

If you are moving into a new home, start with the furniture that solves daily problems: kitchen, wardrobes, bed storage, shoe rack and TV storage. Decorative elements can come after the main storage and movement are right.
