Think by kitchen zone, not only by material name

Kitchen failure usually starts around moisture, poor edge treatment, weak hardware or bad installation. This is why a single material answer is not enough. The sink-base cabinet, hob side, wall unit and tall pantry may need different levels of durability and cost control.

Common material options

MaterialWhere it can workWhat to confirm

Finish should match daily use

Laminate is practical, durable and easier to maintain for many family kitchens. Acrylic gives a glossy premium look but needs more careful handling. Veneer and PU can add warmth and richness, but they are best used where maintenance expectations are clear.

If the kitchen is used heavily every day, choose cleanable and repair-aware finishes before choosing high-gloss drama.

Hardware is part of the material decision

Even good boards feel weak if hinges, channels and fittings are poor. Spend on smooth drawers, soft-close where useful, strong hinges and correctly installed pull-outs. Hardware should support the storage plan, not be added only for brochure value.

How material affects pricing

AlterCraft modular kitchens start at INR 1,200 / sq. ft. for the agreed modular cabinet scope. Material upgrades, premium finishes, hardware systems, accessories, countertop and site changes can change the final quote. Ask for the material specification in writing before comparing prices.

Quote checklist for kitchen material

  • Board type, thickness and grade are mentioned clearly.
  • Sink-base material and protection details are explained.
  • Shutter finish is named by type, not only color.
  • Hardware brand or grade is written where relevant.
  • Accessories and countertop are separated from cabinet scope.
  • Warranty support is explained across eligible products and workmanship scope.

Frequently asked questions

Is plywood better than HDHMR for kitchens?

It depends on the use zone, grade, edge sealing and finish. For moisture-risk areas, specification and detailing matter more than a generic material label.

Which finish is easiest to maintain?

Laminate is usually one of the most practical finishes for daily-use kitchens because it is durable and cleanable.

Should I choose the same material everywhere?

Not always. A zone-wise approach can give better durability and budget control.
