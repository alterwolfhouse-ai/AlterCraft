Why one fixed interior price is usually misleading

Two 2BHK flats can have completely different budgets. One family may need only kitchen and wardrobes, while another may need beds, TV unit, shoe rack, mandir, wall panels, study table and office storage. The same area does not mean the same scope.

AlterCraft’s approach is furniture-led: understand the rooms, sizes, storage needs and finish expectations first, then prepare a measured quote by category.

2BHK versus 3BHK budget thinking

A 2BHK usually needs sharper prioritization because bedrooms and kitchen are compact. Spend first on kitchen durability, wardrobe storage and a practical TV or shoe storage solution. A 3BHK can often support more room-wise customization, such as guest-room storage, dedicated study, larger TV wall or more premium finishes.

What makes a quote go up

  • More cabinet volume, drawers, tall units and lofts.
  • Premium shutters such as acrylic, veneer, PU or special textures.
  • Higher-grade hardware, channels, lift-ups and sliding systems.
  • Wall panels, profile lighting and ceiling coordination.
  • Electrical, plumbing, civil or countertop work added to furniture scope.

How to keep the budget controlled

Start with must-have storage, then add premium finishes selectively. A home can feel elegant with good proportions, practical materials and neat execution. It does not need expensive finish everywhere.

Practical rule: separate fixed essentials from optional upgrades. This makes the final quotation easier to understand and easier to phase if needed.
