Dispute protection for contractors: proof before emotion

When a project dispute starts, emotion arrives before evidence. Contractor Desk is designed around a simple rule: keep proof ready before the fight starts.

Why disputes become expensive

Many contractor disputes are not about skill. They happen because the scope was verbal, extra work was not written, payment stages were unclear, photos were missing, and cash movement was not tied to the job. By the time the contractor wants to argue, the evidence is scattered across WhatsApp, memory and loose bills.

What proof should be locked

Written scope

Work order, quotation, measurements, exclusions and material assumptions.

Payment records

Advance, material amount, labour advance, milestone invoice and pending balance.

Site photos

Before photos, progress photos, damage photos, installation proof and handover photos.

Chat evidence

WhatsApp confirmations, client approvals, change requests and issue discussions.

How the cash ledger helps

A contractor should know whether money went into material, labour, transport, tools, debt, rent, reserve or profit. When all cash is mixed, the owner may feel busy while the business is quietly losing money. Contractor Desk gives each rupee a bucket so the owner can see what is project money and what is real profit.

The right escalation path

The aim is not to fight faster. The aim is to settle with evidence first, then escalate lawfully only when needed. A dispute file should show amount at risk, settlement offer, missing evidence, witness notes and the next lawful action.
