Starting price and scope

AlterCraft currently communicates modular kitchen starting pricing as INR 1,200 / sq. ft. for the agreed modular cabinet scope. This is useful as a starting signal, but it should not be treated as a final all-inclusive project price before measurement.

The right way to read the number is simple: it helps you begin the conversation, and the final quotation is prepared after the cabinet scope, material, hardware, finish, countertop, accessories and site requirements are confirmed. That keeps the pricing clearer and avoids vague promises.

Practical note: no hidden cost is possible only when the scope is written clearly. The quote should say what is included, what is excluded, and what may change after site measurement.

Layout and cabinet volume

A straight kitchen is usually simpler to estimate than an L-shaped, U-shaped or parallel kitchen because the cabinet run is easier to calculate. Corners, tall units, appliance towers, lofts and pantry storage add cabinet volume and hardware. That is why two kitchens with the same floor area can have different budgets.

Straight kitchen

Good for compact flats, rental homes and smaller walls where storage can stay simple.

L-shaped kitchen

Good for corner use, but needs careful planning for corner storage and sink-hob movement.

Parallel kitchen

Good for storage and workflow when both walls are usable and movement space is clear.

U-shaped kitchen

Higher storage potential, but it needs enough movement space and more cabinet planning.

Material and finish

Kitchen material should be chosen by zone. Sink-base cabinets and utility-side storage need more moisture-aware thinking than dry wall cabinets. BWR or BWP plywood options, HDHMR and other engineered boards each make sense in different cases depending on load, exposure and budget.

Finish also changes the estimate. Laminate is practical and easy to maintain. Acrylic gives a glossy premium look. Veneer and PU can make selected areas feel warmer or richer, but they need a more careful budget conversation. The best kitchen is not the one with the most expensive finish; it is the one where the finish matches daily use.

Hardware and accessories

Soft-close hinges, drawer channels, bottle pull-outs, tandem drawers, corner units, pantry systems, lift-up shutters and dustbin units change cost quickly. Spend first where your hands go every day: cutlery, plates, cookware, oil bottles, cleaning supplies and pantry access.

If the budget is limited, avoid filling every cabinet with expensive accessories. A few correct hardware choices can make a kitchen feel premium without making the whole quote heavy.

Site work and hidden cost control

Electrical shifting, plumbing changes, tile height, counter cutouts, chimney position, gas line, appliance sizes and wall condition can all affect the final quotation. This is why site photos and dimensions are useful before the first estimate, but final confirmation should happen after measurement.

Ask every vendor to separate modular cabinet scope from civil, electrical, plumbing, countertop and appliance work. This makes comparison more honest.

Quote checklist before you approve

  • Cabinet material and thickness are mentioned clearly.
  • Shutter finish is written by type, not only by color.
  • Hardware brand or grade is mentioned where relevant.
  • Accessories are itemized instead of bundled vaguely.
  • Countertop, sink, hob and chimney scope is clear.
  • Installation, transport and site work are included or excluded in writing.
