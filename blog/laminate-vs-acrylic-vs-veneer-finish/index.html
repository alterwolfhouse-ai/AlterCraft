Finish Guide

Laminate, acrylic or veneer: which finish should you choose?

The right finish depends on daily use, cleaning habits, budget and the look you want. A finish should be beautiful, but it also has to survive fingerprints, dust, moisture and repairs.

Laminate

Laminate is practical, durable and budget-friendly. It works well for wardrobes, kitchens, study tables and rental furniture because it is easy to maintain.

Acrylic

Acrylic gives a glossy, premium look. It suits modern kitchens and wardrobes, but fingerprints and scratches show more clearly, so cleaning habits matter.

Veneer

Veneer gives a rich natural wood feel. It is best for premium areas where warmth and grain matter more than rough daily handling.
