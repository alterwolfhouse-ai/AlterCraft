Measure the wall before choosing a design

Many TV unit references online are made for large rooms. In a compact flat, heavy panels, deep cabinets and oversized shelves can make the living room feel smaller. Start with wall width, TV size, sofa distance, plug location and router or set-top box position.

Keep storage useful and shallow

Small living rooms usually need remote storage, router space, set-top box access, a few drawers and sometimes concealed cleaning storage. Deep cabinets are not always better. A shallow, clean unit can make the room feel more premium and easier to maintain.

Plan wiring before panels

Cable concealment only works when service access is planned. Do not permanently close every wire path behind decorative panels. Keep access for TV cable, HDMI, router, speakers and future service.

Practical note: take a clear photo of all electrical points before designing the TV wall. This avoids awkward cutouts later.

Material and finish direction

For compact rooms, lighter laminates, warm wood, fluted or CNC accents and soft lighting can work well. Use darker shades in controlled areas rather than across the full wall. If profile lighting is used, make sure it is serviceable and not too harsh for evening viewing.

Checklist before finalizing

  • Wall width, height and TV size are clear.
  • Electrical points and cable routes are mapped.
  • Router, set-top box and speaker access is planned.
  • Panel depth does not block movement.
  • Storage is useful, not only decorative.
  • Finish and warranty support are mentioned in the quote.

Frequently asked questions

Can a small living room have a premium TV wall?

Yes. Keep proportions light, storage clean and wiring hidden but accessible.

Should I add wall panels behind the TV?

Panels can improve the look, but they should not block future wiring access or make the room feel heavy.

Can AlterCraft design TV units with storage?

Yes. TV units can include drawers, shelves, display space, panels, cable access and lighting based on the room.
