Internal Layout

Indian Wardrobe Planning

Wardrobe design for sarees, lehengas, sherwanis and luggage needs a different layout.

Many wardrobe layouts are copied from catalog images. Indian homes often need storage for sarees, occasion wear, luggage, bedding, footwear and bags. That changes the inside of the wardrobe completely.

AlterCraft Guide Indian clothing storage 7 min read

A wardrobe for everyday shirts is not the same as a wardrobe for sarees, lehengas, sherwanis and family luggage. Before choosing finishes, list what the wardrobe must hold. This is the practical starting point for the internal layout.

Sarees need shelf logic, not only hanging

Sarees can be folded and stacked, but deep shelves become messy when the stack is too tall. It is better to plan moderate shelf heights, clear grouping and easy front access. If sarees are used often, keep them near the centre zone instead of burying them high up.

Lehengas and sherwanis need protected hanging

Heavy occasion wear should not be crushed into a short-hanging section. Plan a longer hanging area with enough depth and a smoother access path. If the outfit is rarely used, it can sit away from the daily-wear zone, but it should still be reachable without pulling half the wardrobe out.

Luggage belongs in loft or tall storage

Suitcases, blankets and low-use items should not compete with daily clothes. Use the loft or a tall side section for luggage. This keeps the main wardrobe zones available for clothes, accessories and drawers.

Useful zones for Indian wardrobes

  • Daily wear: centre zone where the hand reaches naturally.
  • Saree shelves: grouped shelves with manageable stack heights.
  • Occasion hanging: longer hanging for lehengas, coats and sherwanis.
  • Drawer stack: waist-height drawers for innerwear, accessories and small items.
  • Loft: suitcases, winter bedding, blankets and rarely used boxes.
  • Accessory strip: hooks, trays or slim shelves for belts, watches and small bags.
AlterCraft tip

Do not let Pinterest decide the inside of your wardrobe. Count your clothing types first. A good Indian wardrobe usually needs a mix of hanging, drawers, folded storage and low-use luggage space.

Door type also matters

If the wardrobe has a lot of occasion wear, full access becomes important. Openable shutters can make the full internal layout visible. Sliding shutters can still work in compact bedrooms, but the internal divisions should match how the shutters overlap. Compare both in the sliding vs openable wardrobe guide.

Quick planning rule

Keep everyday clothes in the easiest zone, occasional outfits in protected hanging, sarees in controlled shelves, and luggage in loft or tall storage. That one decision makes the wardrobe easier to use every day.
