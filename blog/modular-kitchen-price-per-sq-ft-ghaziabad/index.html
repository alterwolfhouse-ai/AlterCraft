When per-sq-ft pricing helps

A starting price helps you decide whether a conversation is realistic. For example, AlterCraft uses INR 1,200 / sq. ft. as a starting point for the agreed modular cabinet scope. This gives buyers a clean first signal before the full measurement and scope discussion.

It helps most when the kitchen is simple: straight wall, clear cabinet run, standard shutters, limited accessories and no major site changes.

Where per-sq-ft pricing can mislead

Per-sq-ft pricing becomes misleading when the quote does not explain what is included. Some quotes include only basic cabinet boxes. Some include shutters but not accessories. Some exclude countertop, sink, chimney, electrical changes, plumbing, transport or installation.

Ask this first: Is this price for cabinet area only, wall area, floor area, running feet, or full project scope? These are not the same.

What should be written in the estimate?

Cabinet scope

Base units, wall units, tall units, lofts and pantry units should be separated.

Material

Board type and moisture-sensitive zones should be mentioned clearly.

Finish

Laminate, acrylic, veneer, PU or mixed finish should not be hidden behind one word: premium.

Hardware

Hinges, channels, lift-ups and accessories should be listed by grade or brand where possible.

Simple example of a better kitchen quote

A clearer kitchen estimate might say: straight kitchen, base cabinets, wall units, selected laminate finish, specified board option, soft-close hardware in selected areas, no countertop included, no civil work included, installation included after measurement.

This kind of quote is easier to compare than a one-line price. It also protects the customer and the execution team because both sides understand what has been agreed.

Questions to ask before approving a kitchen price

  • Which exact cabinet areas are included?
  • Is countertop included or separate?
  • Are sink, hob, chimney and appliance cutouts included?
  • Which hardware is standard and which is optional?
  • Are transport, fitting and finishing included?
  • What changes after site measurement?
